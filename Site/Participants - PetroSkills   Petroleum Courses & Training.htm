FAQs

Who can participate in a PetroSkills training course?
Are there prerequisites or qualifications required to participate in PetroSkills training?
How can I tell which course is right for me?
Do PetroSkills courses give me credit toward continuing education?
How can I participate in a PetroSkills course?

PetroSkills public training is open to anyone who has a need for practical industry knowledge. Participants come from all sizes of operating companies, service companies, and any organization affiliated with the oil and gas industry.

There are no prerequisites or qualifications required to participate. However, to maximize the course experience, you will want to pay particular attention to the Designed For section of each description, as well as how the course fits in its respective Course Progression Map.

The best way to find the course that will meet your needs is to look at the Course Progression Map for your discipline, job function, or subject matter need. Each map will show you how the courses are organized within each discipline by level, from Basic to Specialized. In addition to specific subject matter categories within each discipline, the map will also show you the most relevant inter-disciplinary and cross-training courses from other disciplines.

Yes, PetroSkills courses qualify toward Continuing Education Units (CEU) and Professional Development Hours (PDH). A five day course counts toward 3.2 CEUs and 32 PDHs. Our training group can provide the documentation needed upon completion of the course.

To maximize convenience, PetroSkills public courses are available in over 40 locations around the world, and held multiple times throughout the year. Participants can attend in person, or remotely through our PetroFlex delivery option where you take part in the course in real time, just as if you were in the classroom, from anywhere via your internet connection.
